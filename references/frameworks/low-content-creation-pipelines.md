## When to Use

Use these pipelines when creating any low-content or activity book for KDP. The format-specific workflows cover coloring books (AI and outsourced), journals, and planners. Use the policy section to avoid account termination, and the pricing section to navigate the June 2025 royalty change.

## The Framework

### KDP's Low-Content Classification

Amazon draws a hard line:

- **IS low content:** Notebooks, journals, planners, diaries, blank/lined books -- lose free ISBN, "Look Inside," series creation, expanded distribution
- **Is NOT low content:** Activity books, puzzle books, coloring books -- retain all features

"May 2022 they finally acknowledge low content books and while they don't consider activity books puzzle books or coloring books as low content the rest seems to be fair game." -- Dale L Roberts

Strategic implication: coloring books occupy the strongest position. They get treated like regular books while requiring a fraction of the production effort.

### The Duplicate Interior Minefield

"Over the past few years I've seen and heard of numerous accounts getting suspended and often terminated for using duplicate interiors." -- Dale L Roberts

Rule: every interior you upload must be meaningfully different. Vary line spacing, add different header quotes, change page layouts, include unique graphics. Never reskin.

### The Bleed Dimension Formula

"Regardless of the size of your book you're going to want to add about .125 inches to your width and .25 inches to your height. So as a for instance a 6x9 book the interior is going to be 6.125 by 9.25." -- Dale L Roberts

| Format | Trim Size | Interior with Bleed | Typical Pages |
|---|---|---|---|
| Square coloring book | 8.5 x 8.5 in | 8.625 x 8.75 in | 80-100 |
| Letter-size coloring book | 8.5 x 11 in | 8.625 x 11.25 in | 88 |
| Standard journal | 6 x 9 in | 6.125 x 9.25 in | 120 |
| Planner | 8.5 x 11 in | 8.625 x 11.25 in | 100 |

Margins: 0.375 inches on all four sides for books between 24 and 150 pages with bleed enabled.

### Pipeline 1: AI Coloring Book (Canva Magic Media)

**Tools:** Canva (free or Pro), KDP account
**Cost:** $0

1. Choose trim size (8.5 x 8.5 square is most common) and plan for 50 coloring images + 50 blank backing pages
2. Look up bleed dimensions (8.625 x 8.75 for 8.5 x 8.5)
3. Create Canva design at bleed dimensions, set margin guides at 0.375 inches
4. Duplicate pages to total count (100)
5. Generate images via Canva Apps > Magic Media > Graphics, monoline style
6. Prompt format: "[subject] with intricate pattern inside of it coloring book photo thick lines"
7. Place images on odd pages only (right side of spread); even pages blank
8. Download interior as PDF Print (RGB)
9. Create cover using KDP cover calculator template
10. Upload: paperback, black-and-white interior, white paper, bleed enabled, matte finish

**AI quality warning:** "AI is not perfect, and a lot of times if you look carefully, these images look strange. Many of them have errors like extra fingers or too much grayscale." -- Sean Dollwet. Check every image for artifacts. One-star reviews from AI errors are nearly impossible to recover from.

**Credits:** Free Canva: 50 credits/month. Pro: 500 credits/month. Each generation costs 1 credit and produces 4 images.

### Pipeline 2: Outsourced Coloring Book (Fiverr)

**Cost:** ~$400-$600 for 40 coloring pages

1. Research competitors on Amazon for trim size and page count (standard: 88 pages = 40 coloring + 40 blank + 8 front/back matter)
2. Hire illustrator on Fiverr specifying art style, pages, and trim size
3. Receive files as PNG or PDF
4. Format in Canva at bleed dimensions, images on odd pages only
5. Download interior as PDF Print
6. Give illustrator final page count and trim size for the cover
7. Upload with same settings as AI method

### Pipeline 3: Journal Creation

**Standard format:** 6 x 9 inches, 120 pages, lined interior

"This journal right here is making $349 to $460 every single day so this book alone is doing about $12,000 a month with just eight reviews." -- Sean Dollwet

1. Get cover template from KDP cover calculator (6 x 9, 120 pages)
2. Create cover in Canva -- simple text-based covers work fine
3. Create interior at bleed dimensions (6.125 x 9.25), margins 0.375 inches
4. Build lined page: horizontal line, weight 1, transparency 50%, copy 25 times, "tidy up" to distribute evenly
5. Optionally add quotes, graphics, or Creative Fabrica images to differentiate
6. Duplicate to 120 pages
7. Download as PDF Print
8. Upload, check "low content book" box

### Pipeline 4: Planner Creation

**Standard format:** 8.5 x 11 inches, 100 pages, black-and-white interior

1. Set up Canva at bleed dimensions (8.625 x 11.25), margins 0.375 inches
2. Search Canva for "[year] planner" templates, select free ones
3. Customize heavily: convert to black-and-white, swap fonts, combine page types from multiple templates
4. Build page structure: yearly calendar > monthly spreads (~4 pages each) > goals/notes > daily/weekly planner pages
5. Create cover using KDP template workflow
6. Download as PDF Print
7. Upload with low-content designation

**Budget option:** Fiverr cover + 40-page interior ~$15; cover + 5 unique designs for 80-100 pages ~$45.

### Pricing Under the June 2025 Royalty Change

50% royalty (down from 60%) on print books priced below $9.99. This hits the entire low-content category.

| Format | Typical Launch Price | Target Price with Reviews |
|---|---|---|
| Coloring book | $5.99-$6.99 | $8.99+ |
| Journal | $5.99 | $6.99-$7.99 |
| Planner | $5.99-$7.99 | $9.99+ (escapes reduced rate) |

### Case Study: Coco Rio -- $78,000/Month in Coloring Books

"The brand that we're going to be talking about is Coco Rio... it's doing 78,000 in revenue per month which is absolutely crazy." -- Sean Dollwet

- **Catalog:** 174+ titles, all coloring books
- **Format:** 8.5 x 11, 88 pages per book
- **Team:** Multiple illustrators with varying art styles
- **Pricing:** Start $5.99-$6.99, raise as reviews accumulate, top books at $8.99
- **Top earners:** Beauties in Fairyland ($211-318/day), Life Inside Jar ($180-267/day)
- **Growth strategy:** Reinvest profits into more illustrators, cross-promote with A+ content, build email list via free PDF inside each book
- **What did NOT matter:** broken website, inactive blog, minimal social media. Amazon ecosystem alone powered the business.

## Example

First low-content project: AI coloring book, $0 budget

1. Niche: mandala coloring book for adults (BSR 15,000-50,000 range, self-published competitors on page 1)
2. Format: 8.5 x 8.5 square, 100 pages (50 coloring + 50 blank)
3. Interior: Canva Magic Media, monoline style, all images inspected for AI artifacts
4. Cover: Canva template, genre-matched to top sellers
5. Price: $6.99 launch, target $8.99 after 15+ reviews
6. Launch: Amazon Ads ($3/day), PubBy for reviews
7. Scale: If profitable, hire Fiverr illustrator for second book with unique art style

## Output

A completed low-content project includes:
- Interior PDF at correct bleed dimensions with unique content (no duplicate interiors)
- Cover PDF from KDP template calculator
- Low-content box checked during upload (journals/planners only -- not coloring books)
- Pricing strategy accounting for the June 2025 royalty threshold
- Differentiation plan to avoid duplicate interior termination risk
- Scale strategy (reinvest into illustrators, not template reskins)
