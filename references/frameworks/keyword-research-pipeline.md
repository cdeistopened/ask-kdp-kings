## When to Use

Use this pipeline when preparing keywords for a KDP book upload, optimizing an existing listing's discoverability, or deciding which keyword phrases to target with your title and subtitle. Keywords determine whether Amazon's A9 algorithm shows your book to the right shoppers.

## The Framework

### How Amazon's Keyword System Works

Amazon gives you 7 keyword boxes during book setup, each up to 50 characters. Shoppers never see these fields. The A9 algorithm reads them behind the scenes.

**Key findings from Dave Chesson's 121-author experiment:**

- **Amazon recombines words automatically.** "Fantasy mage dragon war" in one box indexes you for every combination: fantasy mage, dragon war, mage dragon, fantasy war, the full phrase, and more.
- **Repeating words wastes space.** "Putting the same word more than once inside of those boxes or your title or subtitle does not help." -- Dave Chesson
- **More words per box dilutes ranking.** "The more phrases you put inside that box, the lower your rankings will be." -- Dave Chesson
- **Title and subtitle keywords rank higher** than the same words in backend boxes alone.
- **Amazon checks relevance.** Irrelevant keywords can cause you to index for nothing at all.

### Step 1: Start with Amazon Autocomplete

Type a seed keyword into Amazon's search bar (Incognito mode). Apply the alphabet technique:

"The alphabet technique -- type in [your keyword] and then type 'a' and now you have a set of new keywords. 'B' and now you have a new set of keywords. Then you just keep going." -- Sean Dollwet

Use the Amazon Search Suggestion Expander (free Chrome plugin) to automate this process.

### Step 2: Validate Demand

For each keyword candidate, search it on Amazon and check BSR of books on page one.

- Sean's threshold: at least 3 books on page one with Kindle BSR under 30,000
- Dale's scale: "Below a hundred thousand, you're good. Below ten thousand, it's competitive. Below 1,000, you're in for a fist fight."

### Step 3: Validate Competition

Check total search results count in the top-left corner of Amazon results.

"If you can find keywords where the search results is below 3,000, that is typically good." -- Sean Dollwet

Also scan for review counts on page one. Look for keywords where some top books have modest review numbers.

### Step 4: Check for Exact-Match Titles

Search your target keyword and note how many books use that exact phrase in their title. Few competitors using the exact phrase means placing it in yours gives a ranking advantage.

### Step 5: Cross-Format Validation (Optional)

A keyword saturated in Kindle might be wide open in paperback (BSR under 100,000 is good) or audiobook (under 100 total Audible results signals a very open market).

### Step 6: Fill Your 7 Keyword Boxes (Hybrid Strategy)

Chesson's experiment produced a clear recommendation:

"The real answer to this is you should actually do both... Use three to four of the boxes and use those particular phrases inside of it. Now once you've done the three to four boxes, use the rest of them to fill in with other words or phrases that are generally descriptive of what your book is about." -- Dave Chesson

**Boxes 1-4:** Exact target phrases (your highest-converting keywords)
**Boxes 5-7:** Broad descriptive words that capture related searches

### Formatting Rules

- **No commas.** "When do you ever search a company... like I have never done that in the entirety of my internet googling life." -- Jacob Rothenberg
- **No quotation marks.** Quotes trigger Boolean exact match, killing discoverability.
- **No repeated words.** If "meditation" is in your title, skip it in keyword boxes.
- **No competitor brand names, author names, or trademarked terms.**

## Example

**Niche:** Home organization books

Boxes 1-4 (exact phrases):
1. `decluttering your home room by room`
2. `home organization for beginners guide`
3. `minimalist living tips small spaces`
4. `konmari method organizing closet`

Boxes 5-7 (broad descriptive):
5. `tidy clean storage solutions apartment`
6. `simple habits clutter free life`
7. `downsizing simplify belongings family`

No repeated words across boxes. Title keyword ("decluttering") excluded from boxes since it already indexes from the title.

## Output

A completed keyword research package includes:
- 15-30 validated keyword phrases with BSR and competition data
- 7 keyword boxes filled using the hybrid strategy
- Title and subtitle recommendation incorporating the primary keyword
- Keyword-to-category alignment check (1-2 boxes reinforcing category selections)
