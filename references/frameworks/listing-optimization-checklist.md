## When to Use

Use this checklist when building a new listing, auditing an underperforming title, or preparing for a relaunch. Categories, description, and metadata determine whether Amazon shows your book to the right shoppers and whether those shoppers click "Buy."

## The Framework

### The 2023 Category Overhaul

Amazon replaced the old BISAC-based system. You now see actual Amazon categories, select exactly three, and have no appeals process.

"Unlike before where you would just select BISACs and then could go and add up to 10 more categories through a form, Amazon now shows you a list of Amazon categories and you can select three and that's it." -- Dave Chesson

### The Three Category Secrets

**Secret 1: 54% of KDP Categories Are Duplicates**

"Over 54 percent of these KDP categories are actually duplicates of each other." -- Dave Chesson

Use duplicates strategically: find one that maps across multiple browse paths and different parent categories. One selection, maximum coverage.

**Secret 2: 27% Are Ghost Categories**

"Over twenty-seven percent of categories listed on KDP are what I call a ghost category. These are categories where if your book is listed in it you can't get a bestseller tag for it." -- Dave Chesson

Verify before selecting: visit the actual Amazon page. If it lacks a visible category name and browsable tree, it is a ghost. Never waste a pick on one.

**Secret 3: Amazon Overrides Your Selections Using Keywords**

"Amazon looks at [your keywords] and if you aren't using keywords that you would expect a book in that category to use... their algorithm will just remove your book from it." -- Dave Chesson

Your category selections are a request, not a guarantee. Keywords, description, and metadata must align with your chosen categories.

### The 5-Step Category Selection Process

1. **Identify fitting categories** with realistic ranking potential (top books BSR 20,000-80,000)
2. **Check for duplicates** that span different broad parent categories
3. **Screen for ghost categories** by visiting actual Amazon pages
4. **Make your three selections** with full knowledge of the map
5. **Reinforce with keywords** -- dedicate 1-2 of your 7 keyword boxes to category-specific terms

"I now recommend that authors use one or two of their seven Kindle keyword boxes for their category keywords." -- Dave Chesson

### How Amazon Reads Your Book

Amazon's algorithm reads everything -- title, subtitle, keywords, categories, description, and author name. It classifies your book based on all of these signals combined.

**Four misclassification culprits:**
1. **Too-broad keywords** -- "New York hot steamy girl boss romance" is much better than just "romance"
2. **Careless description language** -- "The words in your book description absolutely affect what Amazon sees." -- Dave Chesson
3. **Spammy marketing tactics** -- corrupt Amazon's understanding of your audience
4. **Unintentional metadata overlaps** -- author names or title words that trigger wrong associations

### Title and Subtitle Optimization

"If people have to stop and read the title, read the book cover, and take a couple seconds just to understand what the book is about, then that is a failed book cover." -- Sean Dollwet

**Title rules:** Keep it simple, literal, and instantly readable. Short enough to display fully in search results.

**Subtitle rules:** Explain the genre, format, and audience. Frame the benefit with a concrete promise. Avoid health claims ("heal," "cure") that block Amazon Ads -- use "improve" or "discover" instead.

### The 5-Part Book Description Framework

"The book description has five different parts: you have the hook or the headline, you have the lead section or credibility section, you have the bullet points section... the overcome objection section, and then you have the call to action." -- Sean Dollwet

| Part | Purpose | Approach |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Hook/Headline | Stop the scroll | Bold question or provocative statement |
| 2. Lead/Credibility | Establish authority | Who you are and why you wrote this |
| 3. Bullet Points | Show what's inside | Features and benefits, scannable list |
| 4. Overcome Objections | Address hesitations | "Even if you've never..." |
| 5. Call to Action | Ask for the sale | "Scroll up and click Buy Now" |

Format with bold, italics, and bullet points. Amazon reads this text for classification signals -- audit for unintended keyword associations.

### Author Bio: 5 Keys in 50-100 Words

"Unless you're a household name, you have to build credibility with the reader." -- Zach Diamanti

1. **Punchy first sentence** -- name your books and genres
2. **Area of expertise** -- credentials (nonfiction) or voice/personality (fiction)
3. **Credibility without bragging** -- "award-winning author" not a list of every accolade
4. **Personal touch** -- a hobby or detail that humanizes you
5. **Call to action** -- website link, freebie offer, or social handle

Tailor the bio to each book's audience.

### Red Flag Checklist

- [ ] Subtitle not truncated in search results
- [ ] At least 5 reviews (zero reviews = steep conversion penalty)
- [ ] Description uses 5-part framework (not just 5 sentences)
- [ ] A+ Content with 3-5 modules minimum
- [ ] No health claims that block Amazon Ads
- [ ] Title leads with reader benefit, not author biography
- [ ] Target category #1 bestseller BSR below 400,000

## Example

Self-help book on morning routines:

- **Title:** "The 5 AM Shift"
- **Subtitle:** "A Busy Professional's Guide to Building a Morning Routine That Boosts Energy, Focus, and Productivity in 30 Days"
- **Categories:** (1) Self-Help > Time Management, (2) Health & Wellness > Sleep & Sleep Disorders, (3) Business > Skills -- verified none are ghosts
- **Keywords:** 2 boxes for category reinforcement ("time management professionals," "morning routine productivity"), 5 boxes for buyer phrases
- **Description:** Hook ("What if the first hour of your day determined the next sixteen?") > Credibility > 7 bullet points > Objection handler > CTA

## Output

An optimized listing includes:
- 3 verified (non-ghost, non-redundant) category selections
- Title and subtitle with primary keyword, passing the instant-comprehension test
- 5-part HTML-formatted book description
- 7 keyword boxes (1-2 category-reinforcing, 5 buyer-phrase)
- 50-100 word author bio using the 5-key template
- Red flag checklist cleared
