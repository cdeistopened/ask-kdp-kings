## When to Use

Use this sequence for every new book launch. Amazon gives every new title a 30-day elevated placement window. A structured launch maximizes that audition. Also use the social media and audiobook launch sections when adding those channels to your marketing plan.

## The Framework

### The 30-Day Window

"When you first launch your book on Amazon it has a 30-day new book boost or like a new release boost that Amazon gives you. You get extra exposures for your book." -- Sean Dollwet

A weak launch does not doom your book forever, and a strong one does not guarantee long-term success. The launch gives you a head start. What you do afterward determines whether you keep it.

### Amazon's Five Built-In Promotional Slots

1. **New Release** -- best-selling titles within first 30 days
2. **New and Noteworthy** -- extends to 90 days based on strong initial performance
3. **Most Wished For** -- triggered by wish list adds
4. **Gift Ideas** -- algorithm-selected, especially active during holidays
5. **Frequently Bought Together** -- driven by co-purchase patterns

"The new release feature gives new books a fighting chance where established books dominate the top of the bestseller charts." -- Dale L Roberts

### Phase 1: Pre-Launch (4 Weeks to 2 Weeks Before Publish)

**Review service setup (4 weeks out):**

| Service | Monthly Cost | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| PubBy | ~$20/mo | Review volume (largest member database) |
| Bookbite | Varies (3 tiers) | Multi-format publishers (Audible support) |
| Get Books Reviewed | ~$15/mo (annual) | Budget-conscious publishers |

"You sign up, you start reviewing other people's books, collect some points, and then whenever you're ready, you use those points to get reviews for your book." -- Sean Dollwet

Target: minimum 15 review requests ready for launch day.

**ARC reader collection (2 weeks out):** Email list or social media: "My book launches on [date]. Who wants a free copy in exchange for honest feedback?" Offer copies in exchange for "consideration to leave a review" (Amazon-compliant wording).

**Promo site scheduling (2 weeks out):** Book promotion sites blast your listing to their email lists and social followings. Most use Amazon Associate links and charge nothing.

"Most of these book promotion sites have collected a giant list of emails or social media following that are willing to read books... they most of them won't charge you." -- Dave Chesson

### Phase 2: Free Promotion (Days 1-5)

Activate the 5-day free KDP Select promotion.

"You can set your book on a free promotion on Amazon... have people download your book on Amazon during the free promotion and leave a review which will show up as verified reviews." -- Sean Dollwet

Deploy PubBy/Bookbite points. Follow up with ARC readers. Coordinate promo site blasts.

### Phase 3: The 99-Cent Period (Days 6-20)

Price reverts to $0.99. This is the core of the launch.

"Price your book a little lower than normal. On Amazon's eyes we want to achieve as low of a BSR as possible, and how do you do that? Well the best way is to gather as many sales in terms of volume." -- Sean Dollwet

During this phase: start Amazon Ads, run promo site blasts, continue review requests.

Keep it to 1-2 weeks: "Nobody really does 30 days of launch. That's too long. There's no scarcity." -- Sean Dollwet

Accept short-term losses: "You're investing in your book's future success by ranking your book higher." -- Sean Dollwet

### Phase 4: Full Price (Day 14-21 Onward)

Raise Kindle price to $2.99-$4.99 (or higher for books with 100+ reviews). Continue Amazon Ads at profitable margins.

"After my promotion is over I then start to do Facebook ads and Amazon Marketing Service ads... it keeps my book and kind of the download momentum." -- Dave Chesson

### Launch Pricing by Format

| Phase | Kindle | Paperback (High Content) | Paperback (Low Content) | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Free Promo | $0.00 | N/A | N/A | 5 days |
| 99-Cent Period | $0.99 | $9.99 | $5.99-$6.99 | 1-2 weeks |
| Full Price | $2.99-$4.99 | $12.99-$14.99 | $7.99-$9.99 | Ongoing |

### Social Media as a Free Launch Multiplier

Seven video formats that work:
1. ASMR-style reading with text overlay (no talking needed)
2. Teaching something from the book's topic on camera
3. Showcasing the cover (one video reached 10 million views)
4. Flipping through interior pages
5. Reading the book aloud
6. Coloring page time-lapses (for activity books)
7. AI-generated carousels with tips and background music

"The videos that did the best for him are the easiest ones to create. How easy it is to just film yourself for 10 seconds, put a text on it?" -- Sean Dollwet

Social is a volume play. Most videos get a few thousand views. Post consistently until something catches.

### Audiobook Launch (ACX/Audible)

ACX provides 50 promo codes per title (split US/UK). Each redemption counts as a sale for ranking.

"Every time people redeem a promo code it counts as a sale. So if you have people redeem 50 codes, that's 50 sales, which is enough to typically rank your book on page one." -- Sean Dollwet

Upload codes to BookSprout for automated distribution and review tracking.

## Example

Self-help book launch timeline:
- **Week -4:** Sign up for PubBy, begin reviewing to accumulate points
- **Week -2:** Collect 20 ARC readers via email list, schedule 3 promo sites
- **Day 1-5:** Free KDP Select promo, deploy PubBy points, follow up with ARC readers
- **Day 6-20:** $0.99, start Amazon Ads ($5/day), promo site blasts, TikTok videos daily
- **Day 21+:** Raise to $3.99, optimize ads for profitability, continue social posting
- **Result:** 25 reviews collected, BSR dropped to 8,000 range, organic sales sustaining at 3-5/day

## Output

A completed launch plan includes:
- Pre-launch timeline with review service enrollment dates
- ARC reader outreach copy and contact list
- Promo site submissions scheduled
- Phase-by-phase pricing plan per format
- Amazon Ads budget and keyword targets for Phase 3
- Social media content calendar (7 video formats)
- Post-launch transition plan to full-price profitability
