## When to Use

Use this workflow whenever you are using ChatGPT or any AI tool to draft book content for KDP. The 4-step pipeline protects your account from AI-detection flags, plagiarism claims, and the quality gap that sinks most AI-assisted books. Also use the ChatGPT drafting pipeline when starting a new manuscript from scratch.

## The Framework

### Amazon's AI Content Classification

Amazon draws a clear line:
- **AI-Generated:** Content created by AI, even with substantial modification. Must be disclosed during upload.
- **AI-Assisted:** You write the content yourself, then use AI to edit or polish. No disclosure required.

"In the future I feel like what's going to happen is KDP might start adding a label on the books product page that indicates this book was generated by an AI... and obviously if you have that label on your product page then you're going to lose a ton of sales." -- Sean Dollwet

### The 4-Step Safe Publishing Pipeline

**Step 1: Generate** -- Use ChatGPT to draft content (titles, outlines, body text)

**Step 2: Rephrase + Add Input** -- Run through a rephrasing tool (QuillBot) AND add your own knowledge, stories, and fact-checking

**Step 3: Check for AI & Plagiarism** -- Run through Originality.ai or Grammarly Premium

**Step 4: Publish on KDP** -- Only after steps 2 and 3 are complete

"You're really only adding two extra steps that might take a little bit of time but it is to ensure that your content does not get blocked and also that your account is safe long-term." -- Sean Dollwet

### The ChatGPT Drafting Pipeline

**Phase 1: Reader Pain Point Discovery**

Prompt: "I'm writing a book about [topic]. Please help me list the biggest pain points of the reader that needs to be solved. Tell me what they feel and think and what they want."

"Instead of saying straight up, hey, write me a book on mental toughness, you have to first identify the pain points so that we can write a book around that." -- Sean Dollwet

**Phase 2: Title + Subtitle Generation**

Generate titles and subtitles in separate prompts. The subtitle carries the SEO weight -- your main keyword belongs there.

Subtitle prompt: "Please give me 10 long subtitle ideas about [title]. It should clearly describe the benefits of reading the book. Use power words and numbers to make it attractive. Add the keyword [target keyword] to it."

**Phase 3: Detailed Book Outline**

Prompt: "Create a detailed 8 chapter book outline with three subchapters that have three sections for a book on [topic]. The title is [X]. The subtitle is [Y]."

Add constraints: avoid repeating information, start each chapter with a hook, focus on novel ideas, focus on actionable steps, use markdown formatting.

**Phase 4: Section-by-Section Content Generation**

Target: ~30,000 words total. ~3,750 words per chapter. ~1,250 words per section.

Generate ONE subsection at a time. This is the most important tactical decision.

Prompt: "Please write 1,250 words for Chapter [X] [title], Section [Y.Z] [section title]. Cover these points: [bullet points from outline]. Don't write in a list style. Focus on practical advice and use stories to make the information more personal."

"The key to writing non-fiction with AI is you want to break down the chapter in as much subsection as possible so that you can write in as little section as possible." -- Sean Dollwet

**Why section-by-section?** The context window problem. AI output quality degrades around 2,000-3,000 words. "Context window is essentially how much AI remembers what it wrote in the past... this is right now the biggest issue when it comes to writing a book with AI." -- Sean Dollwet

**Phase 5: Heavy Editing (Non-Negotiable)**

- [ ] Fact-check every claim, statistic, and reference
- [ ] Replace AI-generated anecdotes with personal stories or verified real-world examples
- [ ] Cut fluff and surface-level generalizations
- [ ] Run plagiarism checks (Grammarly Premium or Originality.ai)
- [ ] Rewrite sections that read flat or generic
- [ ] Use iterative prompts to improve weak passages

"Never just copy paste what you generated from ChatGPT into a book and just publish it with no editing no humanizing nothing because that rarely makes a good book. There's a 99% chance that that book is not good enough." -- Sean Dollwet

## Example

Topic: Mental Toughness for Young Athletes

1. **Pain points discovered:** fear of failure, performance anxiety, pressure from parents, comparing themselves to teammates
2. **Title:** "Unbreakable Mindset" / **Subtitle:** "A Young Athlete's Guide to Building Mental Toughness, Overcoming Performance Anxiety, and Winning Under Pressure"
3. **Outline:** 8 chapters, 3 subchapters each, 3 sections per subchapter
4. **Generation:** 24 sections at ~1,250 words each = ~30,000 words
5. **Editing:** 2 full passes -- fact-check + rewrite flat sections + add real sports psychology references
6. **AI check:** Originality.ai score above 85% human-written
7. **Publish:** Disclose as AI-assisted during upload

## Output

A publication-ready manuscript produced through this pipeline includes:
- 30,000+ word manuscript generated section-by-section
- All AI-generated anecdotes replaced with verified examples
- Originality.ai or Grammarly plagiarism check passed
- AI content disclosure decision documented
- Personal stories and domain expertise layered throughout
