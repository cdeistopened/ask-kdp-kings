## When to Use

Use this framework when deciding whether to enroll a title in KDP Select or distribute across multiple platforms. Also use it when planning a transition from exclusive to wide distribution, or when evaluating which alternative platforms fit your catalog.

## The Framework

### The KDP Select Reality Check

KDP Select locks your ebook into 90-day exclusive agreements with Amazon. In exchange: Kindle Unlimited access, Countdown Deals, Free Promotions, Prime Reading eligibility, and 70% royalties in all 13 Amazon regions.

The Global Fund grew from $2.5 million to $52+ million -- a 1,980% increase. But per-page payouts barely moved. Only 10 out of 103 months cracked half a cent per page.

"Amazon knows precisely what they're doing with KDP Select. They're looking to secure authors to their platform so that their competition gets the scraps left over. Most times it's at the detriment of the author." -- Dale L Roberts

### The Genre Rule

"If you write in fiction, I'd highly recommend you really think about using KU and therefore Kindle Select. However, if you're nonfiction, I'd recommend probably not. From market research, those shoppers who sign up for the Kindle Unlimited program are usually fiction readers." -- Dave Chesson

KU books appear to get algorithmic preference -- not because Amazon rigs it, but because KU subscribers convert at higher rates (free checkout vs. purchase), which feeds Amazon's conversion-based algorithm.

### KDP Select Benefits

- Kindle Countdown Deals (70% royalty at $0.99 -- US and UK only)
- 5-Day Free Promotion (reviews + series funnel)
- Prime Reading nominations (~2,500 page reads/month with zero ad spend)
- 70% royalty in Brazil, Japan, Mexico, India (35% without Select)
- ABSR boost from KU checkouts

### KDP Select Hidden Costs

- Locked out of Barnes & Noble, Kobo, Apple Books, all libraries
- Cannot distribute from your own website
- Ineligible for USA Today bestseller list (requires multi-platform data)
- Amazon can change terms at any time

### The Four Alternative Platforms

**Platform 1: Draft2Digital (D2D)**

Free aggregator distributing to Barnes & Noble, Kobo, Apple Books, Tolino, Fable, Amazon, and 7+ library systems. Fee: ~10% of list price. You keep ~60%.

"The part I love most about Draft2Digital is they reach into libraries." -- Dale L Roberts

Key tools: Books2Read universal links, affiliate integration (double-dip on sales), payment splitting for co-authors, Smashwords Store with coupons and bestseller list reporting.

Optimal hybrid: "Put your book in KDP Print and turn off the expanded distribution. Use us for expanded distribution." -- Chris Austin, D2D CEO

**Platform 2: Kobo Writing Life**

Direct publishing for ebooks and audiobooks. 70% royalty above $2.99, 45% below. No delivery fees. Reaches 200+ countries through 24+ retailers including Walmart.

Kobo Plus: reading subscription ($7.99/mo ebooks, $9.99 combined) that requires NO exclusivity. Authors paid based on time consumed, not pages read. Available in 18 regions vs. KDP Select's 12.

"Kobo Writing Life must have seen the lopsided way KDP Select pays authors so they came up with a different system. Instead of being paid by pages read, Kobo Plus pays authors based on the time consumed while reading." -- Dale L Roberts

Library access: direct OverDrive integration to 76,000 libraries worldwide. Library royalty: 50%.

**Platform 3: Lulu**

Oldest POD provider (founded 2002). Widest physical format selection: 16 trim sizes, hardcover with dust jacket, coil/saddle stitch, 4 color modes. Distributes through Ingram Book Group.

Ebook royalties up to 90% on the Lulu Bookstore. Print: 50% of list minus print cost through retail distribution.

**Platform 4: IngramSpark**

Industry-standard distribution reaching 40,000+ retailers, libraries, and wholesalers. Setup fee: $49/title (waived during promotions). Print royalty: 40% of list minus print cost through wholesale channels. Primary advantage: bookstore and library reach that KDP Print cannot match.

### The Transition Framework

**Phase 1: Evaluate** -- Run numbers on current KDP Select revenue (page reads vs. direct sales). If page reads are less than 20% of total revenue, the exclusivity cost is high.

**Phase 2: Opt Out** -- Before the 90-day period auto-renews, uncheck KDP Select enrollment.

**Phase 3: Distribute** -- Upload ePub to D2D (widest retail + library reach) and/or Kobo Writing Life (direct + Kobo Plus). Keep KDP Print active with expanded distribution OFF; use D2D for expanded print.

**Phase 4: Optimize** -- Set platform-specific pricing. Enroll in Kobo Plus (no exclusivity required). Activate D2D library distribution with 2-3x retail pricing.

**Phase 5: Build** -- Create Books2Read universal links for all marketing. Build your own audience (email list, website) so you control the traffic regardless of platform.

### Decision Matrix

| Your Situation | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Fiction, no audience yet | KDP Select (KU readers = built-in discovery) |
| Nonfiction, any stage | Go wide from the start |
| Fiction, established audience | Test wide for 90 days, compare revenue |
| Revenue mostly from direct sales (not page reads) | Go wide -- exclusivity not earning its cost |
| Revenue mostly from page reads | Stay in Select until you build alternative channels |
| Want library distribution | Must go wide (D2D or Kobo) |
| Want USA Today bestseller eligibility | Must go wide |

## Example

Nonfiction publisher with 8 titles, all in KDP Select:
- Page reads: 15% of total revenue -- exclusivity cost is high
- Action: Opt out of Select on next renewal for all 8 titles
- Upload ePubs to D2D (covers Barnes & Noble, Apple, libraries) and Kobo (Kobo Plus enrollment, no exclusivity)
- Keep KDP Print active, turn off expanded distribution, let D2D handle expanded print
- Create Books2Read universal links, replace all Amazon-specific links in marketing
- Result: 5 additional revenue streams per title, library access, USA Today eligibility

## Output

A completed wide vs. exclusive decision includes:
- Revenue split analysis (page reads vs. direct sales as percentage)
- Genre-based recommendation (fiction = Select consideration; nonfiction = wide)
- Selected alternative platforms with rationale
- Transition timeline (opt-out dates, upload schedule)
- Updated marketing links (Books2Read universal links)
- Platform-specific pricing set
