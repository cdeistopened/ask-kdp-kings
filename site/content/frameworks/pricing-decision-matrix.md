## When to Use

Use this matrix when setting prices for any KDP title -- initial launch pricing, post-launch adjustments, or catalog-wide repricing after Amazon's royalty changes. Also use it to evaluate whether KDP Select enrollment makes financial sense for a specific title.

## The Framework

### Ebook Royalty Brackets

| Price Range | Royalty Rate | Delivery Fee | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| $0.99 - $2.98 | 35% | None | Launch promos only |
| $2.99 - $9.99 | 70% | Yes (file-size based) | Primary pricing tier |
| $10.00 - $19.98 | 35% | None | **Dead zone -- avoid** |
| $19.99+ | 35% | None | Premium/specialized only |

"70% royalty is The Sweet Spot on the Amazon platform. They prefer that you price your ebook somewhere between $2.99 and $9.99." -- Dale L Roberts

### The Dead Zone: $10.00 to $19.98

At $9.99 on the 70% tier, your royalty is roughly $6.99. To match that revenue at 35%, you need to price at approximately $19.99 ($19.99 x 0.35 = ~$7.00). Any price between $10.00 and $19.98 earns you less than $9.99 would.

"The break even point... at the 70% model is $9.99 — you get about $6 and some change. The same is going to be said that if you go 35% model, anything that's $19.99 or above is probably going to be the good idea when it comes to the 35% royalty structure." -- Dale L Roberts

### The Global Royalty Penalty

Without KDP Select, you receive only 35% royalties in Brazil, Japan, Mexico, and India -- regardless of your price. KDP Select enrollment grants 70% in all 13 Amazon marketplaces.

"Platforms like Apple Books for Authors, Barnes & Noble Press, Google Play Books, and of course Kobo Writing Life allow for 70% royalty per sale -- period. No asterisk, no delivery fees, no pricing shenanigans." -- Dale L Roberts

### Print Royalty Math (Post-June 2025)

**Royalty = (List Price x Royalty Rate) - Printing Cost**

| Price Range | Royalty Rate |
|---|---|
| $9.99 and above | 60% |
| Below $9.99 | 50% (cut from 60%) |

"This royalty cut doesn't just trim earnings. It pressures entire business models." -- Dale L Roberts

Hardest hit: children's book authors (color interiors + parent price resistance), short nonfiction (naturally priced below $9.99), and low-content publishers (journals, notebooks, coloring books).

### KENPC Page Read Economics

Per-page payouts hover between $0.004 and $0.005. A 200-page book read cover-to-cover earns roughly $0.90. The Global Fund grew from $2.5 million to $52+ million, but per-page rates have barely moved.

"Out of the past 103 months since the beginning of KENPC, only 10 of those months cracked the half cent per page mark." -- Dale L Roberts

"The KENPC payout for KDP Select enrolled books is much akin to boiling a frog." -- Dale L Roberts

Strategy: optimize for direct sales first, treat page-read income as a bonus.

### Audiobook Pricing (ACX/Audible)

You do not set audiobook prices. Audible sets them by audio length. Royalty: 40% exclusive, 25% non-exclusive.

A counterintuitive advantage: Audible members redeem one monthly credit on any book regardless of price, so they choose expensive ones. "More expensive books sell better on Audible just because Audible members get one free credit to redeem for any book of their choice." -- Sean Dollwet

ACX Bounty: $75 per new Audible subscriber who signs up through your book and stays past the trial.

### Three Pricing Strategies

**1. Price Pulsing (Finding Your Sweet Spot)**

Adjust price incrementally to discover what your audience will pay. Launch at $7.99, drop to $2.99 if sales are weak, inch back up as sales build.

"If you use price pulsing, do it only to figure out what your buying audience is willing to spend. You're going to find that sweet spot somewhere in the middle." -- Dale L Roberts

**2. Hotshotting (Rapid Bestseller Badge)**

Drop to $0.99 for a 3-5 day window to spike sales volume and hit bestseller status in your category. Return to regular price. Works best with coordinated social media and email list promotion.

**3. Kindle Countdown Deals (KDP Select Only)**

7-day price drop that maintains the 70% royalty even at the discounted price (US and UK only). The only way to earn 70% on a $0.99 book.

"A Kindle Countdown Deal allows you to drop the price and keep the 70% royalty option, whereas if you just manually dropped the price below $2.99, you'd only earn 35%." -- Dale L Roberts

## Example

Nonfiction self-help book, 200 pages:

- **Ebook:** $4.99 (70% tier) = ~$3.49/sale. Not enrolled in KDP Select (nonfiction audience skews toward direct purchase over KU)
- **Paperback:** $12.99 (above $9.99 threshold for 60% royalty) minus ~$3.50 print cost = ~$4.29/sale
- **Hardcover:** $19.99 = higher royalty per sale, credibility boost
- **Audiobook:** 5-hour narration, Audible sets price ~$14.95, exclusive royalty ~$4.60/sale + $75 bounty potential
- **Launch sequence:** $0.99 for 2 weeks (hotshotting), then price pulse from $2.99 up to $4.99

## Output

A completed pricing decision includes:
- Ebook price positioned within the 70% tier (or justified exception)
- Print price above $9.99 if possible to preserve 60% royalty
- Dead zone avoided ($10.00-$19.98)
- KDP Select enrollment decision with genre-specific rationale
- Launch pricing strategy selected (price pulsing, hotshotting, or countdown deal)
- Audiobook length target for optimal Audible pricing tier
