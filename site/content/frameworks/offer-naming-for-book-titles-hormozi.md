## When to Use

Use this when naming a book (title + subtitle) and you want to go beyond keyword stuffing. The KDP Kings Book Architect skill generates 10 title options based on pain points and keywords. This article gives you the psychological framework for evaluating which title will convert browsers into buyers, using Hormozi's offer naming principles adapted to the Amazon bookshelf.

Also use this when a well-keyworded book with a good cover isn't converting impressions to clicks, or when you need to differentiate your title from 15 competitors with near-identical names.

## The Core Insight

Hormozi's central claim about naming: the name of the offer IS the offer. People don't read your sales page first and then decide. They see the name, form a judgment in under two seconds, and decide whether to look further.

On Amazon, this is amplified. Your book title appears:
- In search results alongside 15+ competitors
- At thumbnail size (the reader can't read your subtitle)
- In "Customers also bought" carousels
- In ad placements with zero supporting context

The title must do the selling before the reader ever sees your description, reviews, or "Look Inside."

## Hormozi's Five Naming Criteria Applied to Books

### 1. Name the Dream Outcome, Not the Topic

**Hormozi principle:** "The name should tell the prospect what they're going to get, not what you're going to do."

**Bad book title:** "Beekeeping: A Complete Guide"
**Better:** "Backyard Bees: Your First Hive to First Harvest in 90 Days"

The first names the topic. The second names what the reader fantasizes about: having a working hive producing honey within a season.

**KDP application:**
- Nonfiction titles should contain the result, not just the subject
- Use the subtitle to add the "how" and "timeframe" — these are the specificity layers Hormozi calls "power qualifiers"
- If your title could be a Wikipedia article heading, it's too generic

### 2. Make It Specific Enough to Be Falsifiable

**Hormozi principle:** Vague promises feel safe but convert poorly. Specific promises feel risky but convert at 3-5x the rate.

"Learn Spanish" vs. "Conversational Spanish in 30 Days: 1,000 Words That Cover 85% of Daily Conversation"

The second title makes a falsifiable claim. The reader can verify whether 1,000 words really cover 85% of daily conversation. That specificity signals confidence, which builds trust, which drives purchases.

**KDP application:**
- Numbers in titles increase click-through rate on Amazon (the KDP Kings data confirms this)
- Timeframes ("in 30 days," "in 5 minutes a day") compress perceived time delay (Hormozi's Value Equation)
- Percentages and quantities ("85%," "1,000 words," "7 habits") signal that the author has organized the knowledge, not just dumped it

### 3. Use the Language of the Buyer, Not the Expert

**Hormozi principle:** "Use the words your prospect uses to describe their problem, not the words you use."

A nutrition expert writes: "Optimizing Glycemic Response Through Macronutrient Timing"
The buyer searches: "stop feeling tired after lunch"

On Amazon, this gap is fatal. The A9 algorithm matches buyer search terms to your title, subtitle, and keywords. If you use expert language, you miss the search queries real buyers type.

**KDP application:**
- Mine Amazon autocomplete for the actual phrases buyers use
- Read negative reviews of competitor books — the language of complaints is the language of desire
- Sean Dollwet's pain point discovery phase (Book Architect Phase 1) surfaces buyer language before you ever name the book

### 4. The Thumbnail Test Is the Naming Test

**Hormozi principle:** Your offer name needs to work at a glance, without explanation.

Sean Dollwet applies this same test to covers: "If people have to stop and read the title, read the book cover, and take a couple seconds just to understand what the book is about, then that is a failed book cover."

At thumbnail size on Amazon search results, only 3-5 words are readable. Your title must communicate the core promise in those 3-5 words. The subtitle (which is visible in text but not on the thumbnail) carries the keywords and specificity.

**KDP application:**
- Title: 3-5 words maximum for thumbnail readability
- Subtitle: 10-20 words with keywords, timeframes, and specificity
- If you can't explain what the book delivers in the title alone, the title is wrong

### 5. Test Before You Commit

**Hormozi principle:** Run the name past people who don't know you. If they can tell you what the offer is and who it's for within 5 seconds, the name works.

On Amazon, you can test titles before publishing:
- Run two Amazon Ads with different titles pointing to the same placeholder listing
- Compare click-through rates over 500+ impressions
- The title with the higher CTR is the winner — this is real buyer data, not opinion

**KDP application:**
- Dave Chesson recommends testing titles through ad CTR before finalizing
- You can also test on social media: post two title options, see which gets more engagement
- Never let the author (or yourself) fall in love with a title before testing it

## The Title-Subtitle Stack as Offer Structure

Think of the book listing the way Hormozi thinks of an offer stack:

| Component | Hormozi Equivalent | Job |
|-----------|-------------------|-----|
| **Title** | Offer Name | Communicate the dream outcome in 3-5 words |
| **Subtitle** | Power Qualifier | Add specificity, timeframe, and keywords |
| **Cover** | Packaging | Signal genre and quality at thumbnail size |
| **Description** | Sales Page | Overcome objections, stack value, call to action |
| **Reviews** | Testimonials | Build perceived likelihood |
| **Price** | Price Anchor | Frame value relative to alternatives |

The title is not metadata. The title is the offer. Get it wrong and no amount of ad spend, reviews, or description optimization will compensate.

## Output

After applying this framework, you should have:
- A title evaluated against all 5 Hormozi naming criteria
- A title that passes the thumbnail test (3-5 words, dream outcome clear)
- A subtitle that carries keywords and specificity without competing with the title
- A testing plan to validate the title with real buyer data before committing
