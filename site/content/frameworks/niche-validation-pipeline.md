## When to Use

Use this pipeline before committing time or money to any book project. A niche passes only when all three validation steps come back positive. Run this for every new book idea, every niche pivot, and every keyword cluster you are considering entering.

## The Framework

### Step 1: Find What People Are Searching For

Amazon's search bar is your primary research tool.

1. Open an Incognito window to strip personalization
2. Select the Kindle Store from the department dropdown
3. Type your idea phrase and study Amazon's autocomplete suggestions
4. Apply the alphabet technique -- append a, b, c, d to expand suggestions further
5. Build a list of 36-48 terms before evaluating any of them

"In order to find, validate, and improve our book idea, we need to do three things. Number one: what are people actually looking for on Amazon? Number two: will people pay for that book? Number three: is the competition too great?" -- Dave Chesson

### Step 2: Confirm Willingness to Pay (BSR Analysis)

For each keyword, search it on Amazon and check the Best Seller Rank of the top results. Use DS Amazon Quick View (free Chrome extension) to see BSR inline.

**BSR-to-Sales Reference Table:**

| BSR Range | Approximate Sales/Day | Signal |
|---|---|---|
| ~10,000 | ~12 sales/day | Strong demand, but competitive |
| 10,000-100,000 | 1-12 sales/day | The sweet spot for new publishers |
| ~100,000 | ~1 sale/day | Minimum viable demand |
| Above 100,000 | Less than 1 sale/day | Likely not enough demand |

"I want to see a rank of anywhere between 10,000 and 100,000. That's about roughly 1 to 12 book sales per day. Now if it goes closer to number one, it's going to be a fist fight to actually get any type of relevance." -- Dale L Roberts

### Step 3: Assess the Competition

**Sean Dollwet's BSR benchmarks for the top 20 books:**

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| All top 20 books | Under 30,000 BSR |
| Books under 5,000 BSR | No more than 5 |
| Most books | 10,000-30,000 BSR (ideal zone) |

"You want to find a niche that is preferably all top 20 books under 30,000 BSR. But no more than five books under 5,000, because if all 20 books are under 5,000 BSR, that'll be too competitive." -- Sean Dollwet

**Dave Chesson's beatable competition signals:**
1. Unprofessional covers among the top sellers
2. Poor titles and subtitles that do not target the keyword
3. Descriptions that are giant blocks of unformatted text
4. Old publish dates (Amazon gives a 30-day ranking boost to new books)
5. Low or negative reviews revealing gaps you can fill

### The Self-Published Litmus Test

The single most reliable signal. When self-published books rank on page one and sell well, you have proof that an indie publisher can succeed using only Amazon organic traffic.

"If there's other self-published books on page one making money, that means their sole traffic source is from Amazon. And if they are able to compete in this keyword, then I should be able to come in and compete as well." -- Sean Dollwet

**How to spot self-published books:** keyword-heavy titles, $2.99-$4.99 price range, stock image covers, publisher field says "Independently Published."

### Four Discovery Methods

1. **Category Drill-Down** -- Browse Amazon Bestsellers > Kindle Store, drill into subcategories, look for repeating topic patterns in the top 25
2. **Reverse-Engineer Successful Publishers** -- Find self-published authors on page one, click their author page, mine their catalog for validated keywords
3. **Manual Research with Free Tools** -- DS Amazon Quick View + Incognito + product count under 1,000 in search results
4. **Paid Tools** -- Publisher Rocket ($97), Book Beam ($17-47/mo), KDSPY ($47) for scaled filtering and search volume estimates

## Example

Starting keyword: "ketogenic diet" (5,000+ search results -- too competitive). Follow a self-published author's page from that search to discover "keto meal prep" (BSR ~174, self-published, $5.99). That author's catalog reveals "Mediterranean diet," "paleo diet for beginners," "anti-inflammatory diet" -- all validated niches with room for new entries.

## Output

A validated niche should produce:
- 5-10 target keywords with BSR data for top 20 results
- Confirmation that self-published books rank on page one
- Competition assessment (cover quality, review counts, publish dates)
- A clear "go/no-go" decision with supporting data
- Ranked list of keywords by opportunity (demand vs. competition ratio)
