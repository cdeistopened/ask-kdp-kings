## When to Use

Use this framework when you need to understand how money flows through the KDP ecosystem -- before publishing your first book, when evaluating whether a new title is worth the investment, or when planning how to multiply revenue from an existing manuscript. This is the foundational business model that every other framework in this system builds on.

## The Framework

### Step 1: Understand the Three Revenue Channels

Every manuscript on KDP generates income through three distinct channels:

1. **Direct sales** -- Ebook or print book purchases with per-unit royalties
2. **Kindle Unlimited page reads** -- Subscribers read your book; you earn per page from a monthly fund (requires KDP Select enrollment)
3. **Format multiplication** -- The same content sold as ebook, paperback, hardcover, audiobook, translation, box set, and bundle

"You can turn one book into seven plus income streams: ebook, paperback, hardcover, audiobook, translations, box sets, bundles." -- Sean Dollwet

### Step 2: Map Your Royalty Brackets

**Ebook royalties:**

| Price Range | Royalty Rate | Example |
|---|---|---|
| $2.99 -- $9.99 | 70% | $4.99 book = ~$3.49/sale |
| Below $2.99 | 35% | $0.99 book = ~$0.35/sale |
| Above $9.99 | 35% | $14.99 book = ~$5.25/sale |

"70% royalty is the sweet spot on the Amazon platform. They prefer that you price your ebook somewhere between $2.99 and $9.99." -- Dale L Roberts

**Print royalties (post-June 2025):**

| Price Range | Royalty Rate |
|---|---|
| $9.99 and above | 60% of list price minus printing cost |
| Below $9.99 | 50% of list price minus printing cost |

**Kindle Unlimited page reads:** Less than half a cent per page. A 200-page book read cover-to-cover earns roughly $0.90. The KDP Select Global Fund reached $57.1 million/month by November 2024.

### Step 3: Calculate Your Break-Even Number

Take your total production cost and divide by your approximate per-unit royalty.

"If your book costs $2,000 to make, the ebook retails for $4.99 so you get roughly $3.49 per sale. For the paperback you price it at $9.99 and get roughly $3.69 per sale. If you sold 574 books you'll break even." -- Dale L Roberts

| Daily Sales | Time to Break Even (574 units) |
|---|---|
| 1 sale/day | ~19 months |
| 2 sales/day | ~10 months |
| 5 sales/day | ~4 months |
| 10 sales/day | ~2 months |

### Step 4: Plan for Portfolio Compounding

A single book is not a business. KDP rewards catalogs.

"Those that make a lot of money in this business publish multiple books, usually more than 20 books. If you're spending years writing that one book, you have a huge disadvantage." -- Sean Dollwet

Each new title lifts the entire catalog through Amazon's "also bought" algorithm and cross-format discovery. Sean Dollwet sold his first book catalog for $820,000 on Empire Flippers.

### Step 5: Budget for Platform Risk

Amazon changes the rules. The ACX audiobook royalty overhaul replaced tiered rates with a flat 40% exclusive / 25% non-exclusive -- overnight. The 2025 print royalty cut followed the same pattern.

"Become self-sufficient. Yes, Amazon KDP is fantastic, but you never want to place all your eggs in one basket. You want to leverage Amazon KDP as a platform to build your author brand." -- Dale L Roberts

Mitigations: build an email list, publish on multiple platforms, and budget for seasonal dips (June through September are historically lean months for book sales).

## Example

A publisher investing $1,500 in a nonfiction book priced at $4.99 (ebook) and $12.99 (paperback):

- Ebook royalty: ~$3.49/sale (70% tier)
- Paperback royalty: ~$4.50/sale (60% tier minus ~$3.30 print cost)
- Break-even: ~375 combined sales (~$1,500 / ~$4.00 blended royalty)
- At 2 sales/day: break-even in ~6 months
- Format multiplication: add hardcover ($18.99), audiobook (ACX), and a Spanish translation to create 5 revenue streams from one manuscript

## Output

A completed KDP Revenue Map should include:
- Per-format royalty calculations for your specific price points
- Break-even number based on your total production investment
- A format multiplication plan (which additional formats to pursue and in what order)
- A 12-month catalog target (number of titles to publish)
- Platform diversification strategy (email list, alternative platforms)
