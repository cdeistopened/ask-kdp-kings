## When to Use

Use this framework when you have published 3+ books and are ready to scale beyond solo production. Covers the transition from doing all the work yourself to directing a team, structuring the business legally, layering revenue streams, and staying compliant with Amazon's enforcement system.

## The Framework

### The Three Pillars of Scaling

Sean Dollwet's framework for reaching six figures, deployed in sequence:

1. **Publish more books** -- outsource production to increase volume and quality
2. **Market your books** -- Amazon Ads plus systematic review acquisition
3. **Build your audience** -- email list and social media to own traffic off-platform

"Publishing is a game of stacking; the more books you have, the more money you make." -- Sean Dollwet

The order matters. You cannot market what does not exist. You cannot build an audience without products to direct them toward.

### The CEO Transition Path

**Phase 1 -- Production Outsourcing:**

| Task | Cost Range | Where |
|---|---|---|
| Book covers (starter) | $15-$30 | Fiverr |
| Book covers (premium) | ~$200 | 99designs |
| Ghostwriting | Varies | The Urban Writers, Upwork |
| Interior formatting | $5-$95 | Fiverr |
| Book descriptions (SEO) | ~$35 | Fiverr |

**Phase 2 -- Marketing Outsourcing:**
- Amazon Ads management (learn first, then train a VA or hire Asteroid)
- Review acquisition through PubBy (membership-based, Amazon TOS compliant)

**Phase 3 -- Audience Outsourcing:**
- Email list setup (AWeber or MailerLite) with outsourced landing pages (~$20 on Fiverr)
- Marketing email sequences

"The goal is to get to the point where you are the CEO overseeing your team and directing them, but not actually doing the work yourself." -- Sean Dollwet

"99% of six or seven-figure publishing businesses have a team of writers." -- Sean Dollwet

### Ten Revenue Streams from a Single Book

1. Ebook royalties (KDP)
2. Paperback royalties (KDP Print)
3. Hardcover royalties (KDP Print)
4. Audiobook royalties (ACX/Audible)
5. KU page reads (KDP Select)
6. IngramSpark distribution (bookstores and libraries)
7. Book bundles (zero extra production cost)
8. Email list growth (captured through books)
9. Lead gen / tripwire / upsell funnel (courses, coaching, VOD)
10. Foreign language editions

### Dale's Three-Layer Funnel

- **Lead Magnet (free):** Downloadable report (nonfiction) or prequel novella (fiction), delivered via link inside the book
- **Tripwire (low-cost):** Next logical upgrade, segments list into cold/warm/hot
- **Upsell (premium):** Collection, coaching, consulting, or video-on-demand course

"If you've got multiple streams of income, that helps you out. But if you only have books as your assets, then you're going to need to make sure that you take your audience with you." -- Dale L Roberts

### LLC Formation

Dave Chesson's three reasons: financial protection (separates personal from business liability), tax benefits (deductions for conferences, research, home office), and legitimacy.

"By setting up an LLC, if your book is attacked by a big publisher or someone with a good lawyer, they can take money from the company, but they can't come after you personally." -- Dave Chesson

**Recommended state:** Wyoming (no state sales tax, low recurring fees).

**Setup checklist:**
1. Define 5-10 year business goals
2. Choose structure (LLC standard for solo publishers)
3. Choose name, check trademark conflicts and domain availability
4. Register (DIY or formation service: LegalZoom, ZenBusiness, Northwest Registered Agent, MyCompanyWorks)
5. Receive: Articles of Organization, Organizational Minutes, Operating Agreement, EIN
6. Update KDP account to publish under the business entity

### Copyright Registration

Copyright exists at creation but must be registered for full protection. File at copyright.gov -- $55 electronic, $85 mail. Register every title generating revenue.

"Many authors believe that once you publish your book on Amazon, you should be good to go in having a good copyright. However, that's actually not the case." -- Dave Chesson

### Compliance: The 12 Termination Triggers

1. Duplicate content (same interior, different covers)
2. Keyword stuffing (pen names that are keyword strings)
3. Misleading content (titles promising what book does not deliver)
4. Multiple accounts under one entity
5. Copying other books (lookalike covers, copycat titles)
6. Trademark violation (publish under unlicensed brands)
7. Metadata manipulation (irrelevant categories for bestseller badges)
8. Plagiarism
9. Review manipulation (fake reviews, suspicious spikes)
10. Low-content books as ebooks
11. Low-quality content (persistent one-star reviews, unedited AI output)
12. Offensive content

"To prevent your account from getting banned, just ask yourself: 'Is what I'm doing right now leading to a good customer experience or a poor one?'" -- Sean Dollwet

**Additional violations:** summary books, price gouging, falsified page reads, bonus content exceeding 10%, unpublish-and-republish gaming, review swaps.

**Running multiple accounts (legal way):** One account per legal entity. Second account requires separate LLC, name, tax ID, phone, email, and bank account.

## Example

Publisher at $3,000/month with 12 titles, doing everything solo:

1. **Draw the CEO line:** List all tasks. Circle the 3 only you can do (niche selection, quality control, strategy). Outsource formatting ($5-$10/project) immediately.
2. **Hire first writer:** Upwork, $2.30/100 words, milestone payments at 10K-word intervals
3. **Scale to 20 titles** over 6 months while outsourcing production
4. **Form Wyoming LLC:** ~$200 total with formation service. Update KDP to publish under LLC.
5. **Register copyrights:** $55/title for all 12 revenue-generating titles
6. **Build funnel:** Lead magnet in every book, email list on MailerLite, first tripwire product
7. **Target:** $8,000-$10,000/month within 12 months, sellable business asset
